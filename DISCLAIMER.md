## On the Occupational Hazards of Putting Mathematics Inside Your Head

*With apologies and gratitude to the late Douglas Adams.*

---

This software places a C60 fullerene inside a virtual reality headset and positions it at approximately the height of your sternum.

That sentence is completely accurate and is the reason you will need to sit down.

---

**What actually happens when you put the headset on:**

A shape appears in front of you. It has twelve pentagons. It will always have twelve pentagons. You cannot remove one. You cannot add a thirteenth. Euler proved this in 1750, chemistry confirmed it in 1985, and your Meta Quest 3 will now render it at 72 frames per second approximately two feet in front of your chest.

The shape does not care that you are standing in your living room. The shape does not care that your coffee is getting cold. The shape has twelve pentagons and intends to keep them.

If you reach out to touch it, your hand will pass through. This is because the shape is made of mathematics, and mathematics has never had a satisfactory relationship with hands.

---

**On the question of VR comfort:**

The developers have made every reasonable effort to ensure that the C60 does not induce motion sickness.

The C60 does not move. You move. This is the fundamental insight of VR development, of Galilean relativity, and of most arguments.

If you experience nausea, the developers recommend removing the headset, drinking water, and considering whether the nausea is physical or philosophical. Both are valid responses to encountering a topological invariant at chest height.

**On the build process:**

The application was compiled using IL2CPP, which converts C# to C++, which is then compiled to ARM64 machine code, which runs on a Snapdragon XR2 Gen 2 inside a plastic headset strapped to your face.

At no point in this pipeline does anyone understand the full stack. This is normal. The developers do not understand the full stack. Qualcomm does not understand the full stack. The stack does not understand the full stack.

The C60 has twelve pentagons regardless.

**On the question of the ADB wall:**

Meta requires a verified developer organization before the headset will accept software from a USB cable. The toggles in the developer settings menu are cosmetic until verification completes. The popup that says "Allow USB debugging" will not appear. The documentation does not mention this clearly.

Every indie VR developer has spent four hours discovering this. The developers of this software spent four hours discovering this. The four hours cannot be refunded. The topology does not offer refunds.
